I’d set it up like a repeatable art direction pipeline so every episode image feels like part of the same brand, but not like the same cover over and over.

The goal

Each weekly image should always read as:

Salt All The Things

WoW-fantasy

playful + epic

about this specific episode

So the system needs two things at once:

Stable brand anchors

Rotating scene formulas

1. Lock the permanent brand anchors

These should almost never change.

Character rules

Big salt elemental = hero mascot, broad-shouldered, imposing, glowing blue eyes, bronze/gold armor.

Baby salt elementals = comic actors, chibi, expressive, mischievous, always doing the funny version of the topic.

All salt elementals keep the same material language:

icy blue crystal bodies

jagged spikes

glowing cyan eyes

bronze/gold armor accents

Prop rules

Use these as your recurring visual “podcast identity” markers:

vintage bronze microphone

salt shaker

glowing spilled salt

occasional treasure, lanterns, wood tables, maps, tools

Lighting rules

dark moody background

cool blue character highlights

warm orange fire/torch accents

high contrast, cinematic rim light

Style rules

painterly fantasy illustration

WoW-inspired environment storytelling

no text in image

no real people

square composition, readable at thumbnail size

That becomes your style bible.

2. Build a small library of scene archetypes

Instead of inventing from scratch every week, we classify episodes into a few reliable artwork types.

Here’s the system I’d recommend.

A. Tavern Talk

Best for:

general discussion episodes

opinion episodes

patch reactions

community talk

Visual formula:

tavern/bar/podcast set

microphone on table

big salt elemental as host in background

babies acting out jokes around the table

Why it works:

instantly reads “podcast”

easy fallback when transcript is broad

B. Delve / Cave Expedition

Best for:

exploration

new zones

systems discussion

mystery/speculation

lore-heavy episodes

Visual formula:

cave, ruins, labyrinth, treasure chamber

big elemental as guide/guardian

babies exploring, arguing over a map, pulling a wagon, triggering traps

Why it works:

matches your fantasy tone well

good for “what’s coming next” episodes

C. Raid / Battle Chaos

Best for:

class balance

boss discussion

raid prep

major patch or expansion launch

high-energy salt episodes

Visual formula:

dramatic combat-ready scene

big elemental in dominant action pose

babies panicking, cheering, casting, carrying loot, spilling salt everywhere

Why it works:

makes big episodes feel important

thumbnail pops well

D. Workshop / Build Room

Best for:

housing

crafting

UI/tools/systems

guild infrastructure

making/planning episodes

Visual formula:

forge room, workshop, construction site, blueprint table

babies building, hammering, misplacing things, copy-paste joke visuals

big elemental supervising or facepalming

Why it works:

perfect for housing/tooling topics

gives visual variety from caves and taverns

E. Lore Vision / Cosmic Tension

Best for:

light vs void

story speculation

character arcs

philosophical discussion

Visual formula:

dramatic magical environment

split lighting: blue/void vs gold/light

big elemental caught between forces

babies reacting to magical phenomena in funny ways

Why it works:

makes lore episodes feel distinct and elevated

F. Auction House / Town Comedy

Best for:

economy

professions

farming

mounts

collectibles

side-content episodes

Visual formula:

market stall, treasure room, cluttered guild hall, mailboxes, item piles

babies selling, hoarding, sorting, fighting over loot

big elemental as merchant, banker, or exhausted overseer

Why it works:

great comedy potential

strong object storytelling

3. Give each episode a “composition recipe”

Once you classify the episode, use a repeatable layout.

A good default recipe:

Foreground

2–3 baby salt elementals doing the funniest topic-specific actions

Midground

key prop cluster: microphone, salt shaker, map, tools, loot, ritual object, etc.

Background

big salt elemental as the anchor silhouette

Lighting accent

one strong warm light source

one magical/cool light source

This keeps the images consistent even when the setting changes.

4. Use topic mapping

Every transcript gets reduced into:

Topic A

Topic B

Topic C

Emotional tone

Then we convert topics into props/actions.

For Episode 6, for example:

Midnight hype → cavern/delve/exploration

housing/building → babies building structure

demonology/class prep → small demons / magical prep energy

tone = excited + funny + speculative

That becomes the image plan.

5. Add recurring “visual gags”

This is where the series starts feeling really alive.

You want a few recurring joke beats that can come back in different contexts:

baby salt hiding in spilled salt

baby salt clinging to the microphone

one baby causing the mess while another looks smug

big elemental facepalming in certain episodes

tiny construction failures

map reading gone wrong

treasure chest or mount-related frustration

babies overreacting to void/light magic

These are like your visual catchphrases.

6. Rotate the archetypes so the feed stays varied

A simple rotation system could be:

General episode → Tavern Talk

Expansion/system speculation → Delve / Cave Expedition

Class/raid focus → Raid / Battle Chaos

Housing/crafting/tools → Workshop / Build Room

Lore-heavy episode → Lore Vision / Cosmic Tension

Economy/mount/profession episode → Auction House / Town Comedy

That gives you consistency without repetition.
